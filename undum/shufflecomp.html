Shufflecomp

by Jed Brockett

click to begin

You enter the woods. There is a white horse here with black fetlocks on the front legs, and a rose tree. You reach up to pick a rose.

You have barely plucked a single rose when a handsome young man shows up. He is about a foot taller than you and impossibly slender.

"Lady, let it alone!", he exclaims. "How dare you pluck my rose, Madam? How dare you break my tree? How dare you come to my forest without my permission?"

  • This is MY forest.
  • I'm so sorry!

You chicken out at the last minute and decide to go home.

The end.

"This forest is mine. My father gave it to me. I will pluck all the flowers I wish, and I will come and go without anyone's permission."

The young man grabs you, pulls you into an embrace and kisses you deeply. You're surprised, but you allow it.

When you wake up, he is gone and the wood has grown dark. You take your rose and venture home.

You stammer an apology. The young man smiles broadly. "Madam, in exchange for a kiss, I would allow you to take all the roses you wish." You blush, but allow him to kiss you.

When you wake up, he is gone and the wood has grown dark. You take your rose and venture home.

Three months later...

Return home. Three months pass. You're miserable, and in your room. You look terrible. You haven't combed your hair. You haven't wanted to venture out, because you've been throwing up each morning and are beginning to put on weight. And worst, you haven't had your period in three months. Serving maid exclaims that you are obviously pregnant. She asks about the father. You are evasive, but tell her that it's not one of your father's knights. Sensing your anxiety, she suggests a bitter herb that you can find in the woods that will terminate the pregnancy. You leap out of bed and return to the woods.

You get dressed, brush your hair and rush back out to the woods. The herb is easy to find, and you pick some.

You've barely picked a single leaf when the young man reappears.

"My darling," he cries," I plead with you. Please do not take that herb. Please do not harm yourself or my child. Why would you wish to do such a thing?"

You respond that if only he would human, you would happily keep the child. But since he cannot marry you, you want it gone.

He assures you that he is, in fact, human, and he tells you his tale.

I would never lie to you, Margaret. My name in Tamlane. I used to be human. I am the son of an earl. But I went hunting one night, and I became very drowsy and fell off my horse. It was the Queen of the Fairies who caught me, and I have lived with her ever since. I am both her companion, and her prisoner. Oh, don't give me that look. It's actally quite lovely. I know neither sickness nor pain. I will never grow old. I can change shape, become as large or as small as I wish. I have never before desired to be mortal again. But tonight is Samhain, and every seven years, the faeries must offer one of their own to the devil in exchange for their continued safety. As I am so young and handsome, I fear that I would be the one sacrificed tonight. You must save me, my love. Tonight, the fairy court will travel across the land. You must wait for us at midnight at the bridge. You must take hold of me and hold tight to me, no matter what. No matter what, Margaret. Promise me this.

He disappears. You go home.

Midnight on Samhain

You bleed to death.
